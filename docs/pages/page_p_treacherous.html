Info

In the second semester of freshman year of DigiPen, teams of CS students port over a custom engine they learn to make in another course and create a game in it. Treacherous Totem was what my team and I came up with.

Compared to the sophomore project class, this was less backend-focused since our engine was pretty much provided to us, and we simply filled in missing portions of code. 90% of development was focused on creating the actual game's content.

This project made me realize I cared about design just as much as programming.

What I Did

The Character Controller

This game's physics was simple. The only "solid" collision in the game was the player against the totem pole (there was no ground to stand on). So, the player's character controller was a bit different from what you'd see in a standard 2D platformer. You could move up and down the totem pole and jump off it; that was it. Other movement types would be given to the player as they progressed.

Gameplay Mechanics

If the player could jump off the totem pole, how would they return to it? This was achieved through our first mechanic: an item the player threw horizontally that, when colliding with the totem pole, set the player's position to the point of collision. That formed the basis for the game loop: an assortment of enemies would attempt to knock the player off the pole, and the player would have to try their best to avoid them with the mechanics they're given.
Other mechanics included:

A slide. In the event an enemy or hazardous projectile got close, the player could slide up or down the totem pole to quickly escape danger.

A double jump. After jumping off the totem pole, the player could jump straight up and in a forward or backward arc. It served as a way to avoid more obstacles off the totem pole.

A throwable item capable of collecting out-of-reach coins that increased your score.

An upward dash. While similar to vertically double jumping, this covered a greater distance.

A short-ranged attack capable of killing enemies that got close to the player.

A horizontal dash that sends the player away from the totem pole, but keeps them invincible throughout and kills anything the player collides with.

Enemies

I made quite a few enemies for this game. From spiders that crawled up the totem pole to a flying cannon that fired spikes onto it. Some enemies served as noise keep the player moving while others promoted use of specific mechanics.
