Not Alone

Workers on a space station have found themselves in a nightmarish scenario. A specimen the research division had been studying escaped its confines and wreaked havoc. With no other option left, the sole survivor of the event must navigate their way through the ruined station, avoiding the wretched creature, and completing a series of objectives to eventually escape.

Info

In the project classes of senior of DigiPen, instructors encourage students to make portfolio pieces. While I went on to make Grapplania for the second semester, I worked with two other programmers on a horror-themed game for the first.

Our game was far from amazing (especially with how difficult the AI programmer made the game's enemy), but one can see the effort the team and I put into it (as long as you use the invincibility glitch).

It ultimately allowed me to work on mechanics and level design in a pure 3D space, stuff I had yet to do at DigiPen.

What I Did

The Character Controller

Nothing too much to say here. I had no experience making a first-person, 3D character controller, so I really wanted to make one. I implemented standard movement, camera control, sprinting, and crouching.

Pushing Mechanic

I wanted to add some puzzle elements to the game, and that came in the form of object pushing. When close to a pushable object, the player can clamp onto it, and push/pull it relative to where they grabbed the object from. Unfortunately, the mechanic was fairly underutilized and testers found it a bit clunky the few times they used it. However, it did give me insight on how I'd be able to better the mechanic in future projects.

Level Design

I had actually been taking a 3D level design course alongside this project course. If I learned something new, I'd see if I could apply it to this game's level.

I started the player out in a small, linear area to gradually teach them the the mechanics of the game, then put them in a larger, more open area (which included the AI enemy that would actively seek them). The game loop has players exploring the space station collecting items and performing tasks to get an escape pod functioning. Interactables were evenly scattered to promote exploration, a bit of backtracking, and ensure the player would consistently encounter the enemy.
