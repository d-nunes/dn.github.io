Quest Design

Wanting to continue studying level design, for my final semester of DigiPen (after the 3D Level Design course I took), I signed up for an experimental "Mission and Quest Design" course. Being experimental, it was the first time the course would be taught, and I (as well as a few others) served as testers for it.
The course was a little rough around the edges, but the content was quite infromative and gave me a chance to work on more 3D levels.

Project A

Fetch Mission

The first real project of the course was to create a simple fetch mission given some requirements (i.e. collect 5 artifacts, have a boss fight, etc.) while using a free asset pack provided by Unity. Even with the assets, the level looks and feels like a whitebox when playing or watching it, but that was to be expected. The course was meant to emulate the role of an actual mission/quest designer, who'd create the general structure for the level, adding many temporary elements to it (basic cutscenes, decorations, etc.) for the sake of testing.

Project B

Multi-Mission Level

The final project for the class was to create a full-on gameplay experience based on the story of Little Red Riding Hood. This project was more involved than the previous, as I had to implement 4 missions (defense, fetch, puzzle/explore, and escort) in the level, and ensure the narrative was properly established (through the use of cutscenes).
