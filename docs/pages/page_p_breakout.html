Breakout (Game Feel)

One particular design course at DigiPen had us students take classic arcade games (Pong, Breakout, etc.) and add lots of feedback to enhance the game feel. The base projects we were given contained the absolute bare minimum: a playable game with no audio, color, nor animations, and it was the student's responsibility to make it more appealing.

Juxtaposition

Here are two videos. The left one is gameplay of the base project I started out with. The right one is the finished product.

You can probably tell what I've added, but here's a full list of my additions:

  • Tweening on many elements
    • Player paddle when it hits the ball
    • Bricks as they spawn in
    • Score as the value increases
    • Extra lives as the player gradually acquires them
  • Particles
    • Trail on the ball as it travels
    • Bricks when they're hit
    • Ball when it hits a wall
    • Ball when it hits the paddle after a streak
  • Numerous sound effects
    • Most notably, a sound that increases in pitch as the player maintains a streak
  • Freezing that lasts a single frame when the ball hits a brick (adds more "oomph" to the hit)
  • Camera shake when the ball hits a brick (adds even more "oomph" to the hit)
  • Score counts up rather than statically change to whatever the new value is
  • Extra lives that fill radially till the player earns enough points
  • Ball changes color as their streak increases
  • Dynamic backgrounds that change color depending on the player's streak
  • A deadlier-looking kill zone for the ball
  • A fancy death animation that isn't just the ball popping back on the paddle
  • A more impactful game over sequence
